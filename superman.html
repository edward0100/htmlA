Superman

In some comics, Superman’s speed is depicted as incredible but finite, allowing him to outrun most opponents, but not necessarily faster than light (e.g., Silver Age comics). In other comics, Superman’s speed is shown to be virtually unlimited, allowing him to break the laws of physics and move at incredible velocities, including faster-than-light speeds (e.g., modern comics like Grant Morrison’s JLA #21).

The Flash’s connection to the Speed Force gives him an advantage over Superman in terms of raw speed, as he can tap into this energy and manipulate time and reality to achieve incredible velocities. Superman’s speed has been estimated to be around 186,000 miles per second, with the potential to reach speeds a thousand times higher if needed. His reaction time is also incredibly fast, measured in attoseconds (1×10−18 of a second).
